Steak

Description:

You've had steak before, but have you ever had steak before? Brought up to temperature in the oven before searing and basting with garlic and fresh herbs, this will be the best steak of your life.

Ingredients

  • 1 thick rib eye steak, 2 in (5 cm) preferably USDA prime
  • 1 teaspoon salt and pepper, to taste
  • 3 tablespoons canola oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and smashed
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°F/95°C.
  2. Pat the steak dry with a paper towel, and generously season all sides of the steak with salt and pepper.
  3. Transfer to a wire rack on top of a baking sheet, and bake for about 45 minutes to an hour, until the internal temperature reads about 125˚F/50˚C for medium-rare. Adjust the bake time if you like your steak more rare or well-done, but your goal will be to take the steak out about 10˚ before your goal temperature.
  4. Heat the canola oil in a pan over high heat until smoking. Do not use olive oil, as its smoke point is significantly lower than that of canola oil and will smoke before reaching the desired cooking temperature.
  5. Sear the steak for 1 minute on one side, then flip.
  6. Add the butter, garlic, rosemary, and thyme, and swirl around the pan. Transfer the garlic and herbs on top of the steak and baste the steak with the butter using a large spoon.
  7. Baste for about 1 minute, then flip the steak with tongs and baste the other side for about 15 seconds.
  8. Turn the steak on its side and cook to render off any excess fat.
  9. Enjoy!
